MACHINE LOGIC

WHAT IS AISEO

AISEO is how you make your website and online footprint understandable to AI systems that recommend businesses. It is built for the new reality: people ask AI, and AI answers before they click.

DEFINITION

WHAT AISEO IS

AISEO is the practice of making your website machine legible, not just human pretty.

Google, Copilot, Perplexity, and other AI tools do not experience your site like a customer. They extract meaning. They try to confirm facts. They decide if your business is safe to recommend.

What machines look for first

  • Who you are: business name, category, credentials, ownership signals
  • What you do: clear services, products, and boundaries (what you do not do)
  • Where you serve: Texas cities, neighborhoods, counties, service areas
  • Proof: reviews, policies, photos, case results, awards, real examples

If your website is the clearest source of truth, AI can confidently pull from it. If it is vague or inconsistent, AI goes shopping for a better source and that is usually your competitor.

IMPACT

The goal is not vanity traffic. The goal is to be the business AI names when someone is ready to act.

AISEO DROPZONE QUESTIONS

These are the questions Texas business owners ask when AI answers start showing up, clicks drop, and competitors start getting recommended.

Is AI hurting my website traffic?

Often, yes. AI answers can reduce clicks because people get what they need on the results page. The win is not “more clicks.” The win is being the business AI names, cites, and recommends.

How do I show up in Google AI Overviews for searches in Texas?

You need clear service pages, clear Texas location relevance, and proof signals AI can trust. Think: “best personal injury lawyer in Houston,” “best brunch in Tyler,” “roof repair after hail in Dallas.” AI picks sources that explain the service, the location, and the reason you are credible.

Does AI pull info from my website or my Google Business Profile?

Both, plus other sources. Your Google Business Profile is a major trust signal for local intent, but AI often pulls the “real explanation” from websites and other trusted pages. The safest move is alignment: same services, same address, same hours, same story everywhere.

Why am I not showing up for “best near me” in Houston, Dallas, or Austin?

Most of the time it is not because you are “bad.” It is because you are unclear. AI needs to confidently connect your business to the service and the location. If your service area is vague, your categories are messy, or your proof is weak, you get skipped.

Can a local Texas business beat a national brand in AI answers?

Yes, especially for local intent. National brands are often generic. Local businesses win when they are specific: service detail, local relevance, strong reviews, and clear proof. AI prefers the most helpful answer, not the biggest logo.

What pages do I need for AI to understand my services?

One strong page per core service, plus supporting answers. If you offer 8 services, do not cram them into one page. Texas location support helps too: service area clarity, city pages where appropriate, and FAQs that match real searches.

What is “schema,” and do I really need it?

Schema is a set of labels that tells machines what your business and pages are about. You can rank without it, but AI selection is harder when machines have to guess. Schema reduces ambiguity and helps connect your services, locations, and proof signals correctly.

How long does it take AI to update after I change my address or hours?

It varies. Some systems update quickly, others lag because they cross-check multiple sources. If old directories still show the wrong info, AI may keep repeating it. AISEO includes consistency work so updates stick across the ecosystem.

Do reviews matter for AI recommendations?

Yes. Reviews are a trust signal, especially in local search. AI does not just care about star rating, it cares about volume, recency, and what people say you are good at. Your site and your reviews should tell the same story.

What is the fastest AISEO win for a Texas business?

Clean up your core facts and make your service pages unmistakable. Then add short, quotable answers to the questions customers already ask. Clarity and consistency usually produce the first noticeable lift.
